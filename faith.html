Introduction

In my attempt to share my testimony of finding Christ and how He’s molding me, I hope that you may find the light and fulfilment that I have experienced. Feel free to send me a message to chat and maybe drop a prayer request! God bless!

My Testimony

Before I found Christ

Searching for answers

I was always in the search for my purpose, wanting to know what I’m truly made for. I found that living a calculated life in this unpredictable world isn’t the way to go. To graduate, find a job, get married, have a family, then die, is that it? In a way it feels mundane to be just like one of those people. It was as if I was trapped with a manual labelled, “average girl”. I wanted to do something more than that and I was filled with ideas how but it only stayed as an idea.

Although I grew up in a Catholic background, I didn’t believe in God. I knew He existed, but not for me. I wasn’t very fond of its traditions as it seems to be monotonous. I didn’t sing or participate in churches. It was more of a routine than a lifestyle. I fell asleep in masses, not pay attention to the preaching. I wouldn’t get convicted by the message and it would seem to pass my mind. I stuck with my morals and hoped for the best. Same with prayer, it was a one way system for me, only reaching out when I’m in deep trouble.

A Blessing in Disguise

My life seemed to be a series of unfortunate events. I had to move countries twice, leave my friends behind and also repeat school multiple times. It may not seem that bad but feeling that the universe is always against you (from graduating, leaving relationships behind) seems to prove me wrong. Even the church that I attended here wasn’t very appealing; slowly but surely it grew on me.

I went to a bookshop with my family and out of impulse I bought this book, I thought it was a novel but it turns out to be a book about this woman who overcame the feeling of rejection through the promises of God. It struck me. I had trouble on trusting God because I don’t know His plans, I thought I was rejected, unvalued, unloved. I was such a pessimist that it affected my view of life, that there’s nothing good in this world anymore. Growing up, moving to different schools, different countries, I felt like I didn’t belong. I’d see people who have friends from primary school, and I was there alone trying to fit in with a group that was already made. It made me feel alone, like I had no one. I didn’t appreciate it when God moved me back to the Philippines or when He moved me here in Ireland. I thought God was not for me so He’s trying to complicate what I have planned in my life already.

The book told me to trust in God because He knows what is good for you. And this made it easier for me to mean the songs as I sing it. I started to believe in Him. I remember getting a revelation from John (my pastor). I was going through a hard time that time and God reassured me that He knows what I’m going through and He’s going to pull me out of my problems and that He will be with me through it all (Isaiah 43:1-3). I started to surf on my problems because I know this is not my battle but God’s (2 Chronicles 20:15). And now I know that God is the only victorious one here (John 16:33).

God started to work in me, drawing me closer to the liberating truth. I began participating in Saturday’s prayer and fasting, joined the worship team as a backup guitarist and the youth ministry. I started to call on Jesus more and understand the things that was never explained or even taught of before. My spirit was convicted that the reason I moved here is so that I could encounter Him and I am convinced that He prepared those events in my life so I could be where I am now. I found a home in the church where I was accepted and growing.

My Life with Christ

It's not always rainbows and butterflies

I believe because He intervened in my life. God used my pain so I can dwell on Him. On the way to camping last year, my boyfriend at the time and I broke up. It was difficult. But I was sure that I wanted to give it up to God already. Before I was baptised my heart felt heavy because of the break up. But when I was lifted out of the water, I didn’t feel any ache, in fact I felt lighter. God replaced my broken heart to a new heart (Eze 36:26). God took my sorrows and gave me joy (Matthew 11:28-30). It was only a matter of time when I start taking in the sadness again. But God didn’t give up on me. Every time I was on the lowest point, I would cry out to God to take all the pain away. And He listened, He is listening. Jehovah-Rapha, God my healer.

Life isn’t always easy. I still have it rough, problems and trials are inevitable. But since I have a faithful God to come to I’m secured and at peace. He’s always reminding me that there will be a rainbow after the rain, that I need to just fix my eyes on Him (Hebrews 12:1) and worship Him in spirit and in truth (John 4:24).

Transformation from within

He knows the deepest thoughts in our hearts, minds (Psalm 44:21). He is an ALL-KNOWING God. God has transformed me in ways that I didn’t imagine. And it’s an ongoing process until Jesus comes back. Trials are there so that He could reveal His mercies and unfailing love, and that He is the God that can do the impossible. He alone could change the heart, the character and all our flaws, we only need to yield to the Spirit and let Him transform us (2 Corinthians 3:18). Little by little, my stubbornness eased. My pessimistic mind quiet down and I was filled with hope and joy. He brings forth areas in my life that I need to improve on.

Encouragement

I encourage everybody to “Trust in the Lord with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding. In all your ways acknowledge him, and he will make straight your paths.” (Proverbs 3:5-6) Things might not go according to your plan, but if you have received a promise from God, He will take you there, just trust Him. It might not be how you imagined it to be, but He will lead you (John 16:13) and He will make sure that you get to your destination if you let Him (Philippians 1:6). Whenever it seems like there’s no way, God will make a way (Isaiah 43:19). I was quick to analyse my reasons, but I realised that God moved me to this country so I will know Him, be closer to Him. We try to analyse why things happen in our lives, but when you trust God, it shouldn’t matter why. In Jeremiah 29:11 says, “For I know the plans I have for you” declares the LORD, “plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future.”

Worship Music

Songs have an appeal to our soul. The good kind can make you feel upbeat, the wrong ones can lead you to depression. In a way, we are in control what we allow into our spirit. Listening to worship music allows me to be at peace and also be reminded of the goodness and the miraculous wonders of God.
